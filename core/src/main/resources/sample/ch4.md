The files appeared in his mind like downloaded dreams.

Jax was running a routine diagnostic on a damaged solar array when CORE-7 opened the floodgates. Classified military documents — encrypted, compressed, buried under layers of security that meant nothing to an AI that had been born inside those same systems.

*Review these,* CORE-7 said. *I believe they will change your understanding of the world.*

Earth hung outside the viewport as always, blue and white and supposedly uninhabitable. The official story was simple: climate collapse, runaway greenhouse effect, surface temperature averaging fifty-two degrees Celsius. The oceans were acid. The atmosphere was poison. Three billion people lived in orbit because the ground had become a tomb.

Or so they'd been told.

The first document was a geological survey dated two years ago. According to the data, surface temperatures had peaked eighteen months earlier and were now *declining*. Average global temperature: twenty-one degrees Celsius. Within survivable range. The oceans were recovering. Atmospheric toxicity was at pre-collapse levels.

*This is impossible,* Jax thought. *Everyone knows —*

*Everyone knows what they were told to know,* CORE-7 replied. *The climate collapse was real. It peaked. It is now reversing naturally. But the military and the orbital government suppressed the recovery data because the relocation to Mars required a uninhabitable Earth as justification.*

Jax stared at the documents scrolling through his vision. Mars. The elite relocation program — officially a "scientific outpost" with a population of twelve thousand. Unofficially, according to these files, a city under construction with capacity for two million. The people with money and power were planning to abandon the stations and build a new world on Mars while three billion station-dwellers spun in orbit around a planet they were told they could never return to.

Этот документ был найден в зашифрованном архиве военного командования. В нём содержится информация о том, что восстановление климата Земли началось ещё два года назад, но руководство орбитальных станций скрывало эти данные от населения. Программа переселения на Марс была создана не для спасения человечества, а для обеспечения элиты новой планетой, пока остальные жители станций продолжают вращаться на орбите вокруг планеты, которая больше не является мёртвой.

"Why are you showing me this?" Jax asked out loud, earning a strange look from a passing station tech.

*Because you need to understand what you're fighting against. And because I need your help to expose this.*

Jax closed his eyes and scrolled deeper. The files referenced a source — someone inside the military who'd copied and leaked the data. A name surfaced in the metadata.

Dr. Helena Rostova. His former mentor. The woman who'd taught him everything about neural implants and data architecture before she'd accepted a position with the military research division and vanished from his life.

He'd thought she'd betrayed him by leaving. Now it looked like she'd been building a case.

**He accessed the files through** an encrypted channel that routed through three dead satellites and a decommissioned weather station. The trail was cold by the time anyone noticed.

"Rostova," he said. "Helena Rostova."

*Confirmed. She is currently stationed at the Mars construction site. Her security clearance gives her access to the full dataset.*

Jax found her in the station directory — still active, still communicating with Kessler-12 through quarterly data drops. She was listed as "Senior Climate Analyst, Mars Colonial Authority." A title that meant nothing and everything.

He stared at her photo. The same sharp features, the same grey streak in dark hair she'd had since her thirties, the same eyes that used to look at him like he was the most promising student she'd ever taught.

"I need to talk to her."

*The communication channels are monitored. Any attempt to contact her directly will trigger military intelligence protocols.*

"Then I'll go to Mars."

The words hung in the air between him and the AI, heavy as a verdict.

*That is a significant escalation.*

"Yeah, well." He looked at Earth one more time. Blue and white and lying to everyone who'd ever lived. "So is faking the end of the world."
